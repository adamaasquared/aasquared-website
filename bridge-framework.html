Step 01 — Benchmark

Know what you don't know.

The Benchmark Audit pressure-tests every US assumption against European reality before a euro is spent. Consumer expectations, platform behaviour, brand awareness levels, and competitive intensity all vary dramatically across markets. What your US performance data tells you about CAC, LTV, and conversion rates may be directionally useless on day one in Berlin or Barcelona.

The audit works through five dimensions. Each assumption is classified as one of three outcomes:

  • Portable / Adapt / Rebuild classification for each assumption
  • Prioritised list of what to revisit before market selection or budget commitment
R
Step 02 — Rank

Not all markets deserve your attention first.

Market prioritisation should be driven by the intersection of addressable audience size, category penetration, media cost structures, and competitive density — not instinct, founder preference, or which market feels familiar. The Rank scoring model gives you a defensible, media-informed sequence you can present to a board and actually defend.

Each candidate market is scored 1–5 across five variables. A score of 5 is highly favourable; 1 is high risk or low opportunity.

1Addressable Audience1–5
2Media Cost Efficiency & Entry Threshold1–5
3Competitive White Space1–5
4Language & Creative Complexity1–5
5Regulatory Environment1–5
Deliverables
  • Scored market matrix across all five variables (out of 25)
  • Prioritised entry sequence with board-ready rationale
  • Assumption flags and reassessment triggers per market

D
Step 04 — Design

Structure that compounds, not a launch that evaporates.

Speed without structure is how you burn a European budget in 90 days and have nothing to show for it. The Design step selects the right rollout model for this company's budget, internal capacity, and board expectations — and sequences markets and channels so that each phase generates learnings that inform the next.

Low Complexity Depth-First One market done properly before the next
Recommended Pilot & Parallel One market deep, one or two markets light
High Complexity Breadth-First Multiple markets simultaneously
Seven markets at invisible spend levels is not traction. Two markets with proven CAC, a validated channel mix, and a repeatable playbook is.
Deliverables
  • Agreed rollout model with diagnostic rationale
  • Phased rollout blueprint and learning agenda
  • Budget sequencing model across phases and markets
G
Step 05 — Govern

Accountability before activity.

European expansion often means new agency relationships — local buyers, regional networks, specialist partners. Without the right governance structure from day one, you get fragmented accountability, misaligned incentives, and reporting that doesn't connect to business objectives. The Govern step designs the agency model to match how the company actually operates.

Entry Stage Centralised One agency, pan-European scope
Recommended at Scale Hybrid Thin central layer, local execution
Mature Stage Localised Separate agencies per market
The agency model that works is the one that reflects how the company itself is organised. Design the structure to match the internal reality — then plan explicitly for how both evolve together.
Deliverables
  • Agency model recommendation for entry stage
  • Target model at scale with trigger for evolution
  • Briefing structure and accountability framework
E

Measure what actually happened, not what you hoped did.

Your US measurement stack may not translate. The Evaluate step surfaces four realities US companies consistently aren't ready for — and establishes the measurement infrastructure that needs to be in place before significant budget is deployed.
